Domain: AI for Economic Empowerment & Decision Intelligence  
Problem Statement 
In Malaysia and the rest of the world, many individuals, businesses, and communities face  challenges in making timely and informed decisions due to fragmented data, limited  analytical capabilities, and reliance on manual or intuition-based processes. 
Participants are required to build an AI-powered decision intelligence system, where Z.AI’s  GLM (General Language Model) plays a central role in analysing information, generating  insights, and supporting decision-making. 
The solution must demonstrate how Z.AI’s GLM enables the following points. ∙ Interpretation of structured and unstructured data 
∙ Context-aware reasoning and insight generation 
∙ Recommendation of actions or strategies 
∙ Explanation of decisions in a clear and user-understandable manner 
The system should help users optimise outcomes, such as improving productivity, reducing  costs, or increasing revenue. If the GLM component is removed, the system should no longer  be able to generate meaningful insights or support decision-making effectively. 
Key Expectations 
While submissions can include other features, they must demonstrate the following points. 
∙ Decision-making capability, not just automation, with a product for purposes  including but not limited to a recommendation system, forecasting or predictive  modelling, and optimisation or trade-off analysis 
∙ Quantifiable impact, including but not limited to time saved, cost reduction, and  revenue improvement 
∙ Clear target user group and realistic use case 
∙ Basic validation (scenario-based, simulated, or data-driven) 
Example Directions 
Participants may explore solutions such as: 
∙ Decision-support tools for SMEs (pricing, cost control, inventory optimisation) ∙ AI systems for gig workers to optimise task selection, scheduling, and income ∙ Financial intelligence platforms for budgeting, forecasting, and expense optimisation ∙ Community-level tools for resource allocation, demand prediction, or operational  planning
Submissions should demonstrate how AI enables users to make smarter, data-informed  decisions, resulting in measurable improvements in economic outcomes.
